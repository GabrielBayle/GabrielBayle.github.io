About the project

EXPLAIN (Experimental Platform for Large-scale Investigations) is an initiative supported by the DGRI (Direction Générale de la Recherche et de l'Innovation) and the French Ministry of Higher Education and Research. It aims to transform experimental practices in France by facilitating interdisciplinary collaborations and large-scale experiments.

A first survey of experimental researchers in France identified the main obstacles to conducting experimental research: access to a sufficiently large subject pool, difficulty targeting specific populations, lack of staff to manage experiments, technical constraints related to subject payment, and the high cost of existing commercial platforms.

EXPLAIN's response is to develop a shared platform enabling the pooling of subject pools across volunteer laboratories — allowing experiments to be conducted at greater scale, both online and in the lab. This tool would also serve as a strategic lever to strengthen funding applications (ANR, ERC, etc.) and contribute to open science and reproducibility.

Key objectives

1Pool subject databases across volunteer laboratories to enable large-scale experiments
2Facilitate resource sharing and technical infrastructure between labs
3Support open science, preregistration, and reproducibility practices
4Provide training modules, bibliographic resources, and best practices for experimentalists
5Enable classroom experiments for teaching purposes
