About the project

EXPLAIN (Experimental Platform for Large-scale Investigations) is an initiative supported by the DGRI (Direction Générale de la Recherche et de l'Innovation) and the French Ministry of Higher Education and Research. The project grew out of a national consultation of the French experimental research community, which identified a shared need for a new collective infrastructure to support the conduct of experiments at greater scale.

Yet experimental social science now faces new challenges: statistical power and sample size requirements are growing, replication and meta-science have become central concerns, and data quality issues such as botting and AI-generated responses are increasingly difficult to control at the scale of individual laboratories.

EXPLAIN's response is to develop a new shared experimental infrastructure that pools volunteer subject databases from partner laboratories across France into a unified national platform, while preserving each laboratory's local autonomy. This gives researchers access to tens of thousands of potential participants, with stronger quality controls, harmonised ethical practices, and significantly reduced recruitment costs.

Beyond its role as a shared infrastructure, EXPLAIN is designed to serve as a hub for large-scale meta-science projects, including multi-lab experiments, replications, and pre-registered collective studies, starting in France with the prospect of future international partnerships.

Key objectives

1Pool volunteer subject databases across partner laboratories into a unified national platform, while preserving each lab's local autonomy
2Guarantee recruitment integrity and enable access to larger, more diverse and representative samples
3Reduce experimental costs by offering a publicly funded alternative to existing solutions
4Harmonise ethical and regulatory practices across labs, including GDPR compliance
5Serve as a hub for large-scale meta-science: multi-lab experiments, replications, and pre-registered collective studies in France and internationally
